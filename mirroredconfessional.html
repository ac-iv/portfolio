the mirrored confessional

confession as confrontation with the self

the mirrored confessional explores the fragile space between identity, religion, and self-revelation. this installation transforms the traditional act of confession into an intimate confrontation with the self, asking visitors to sit with their own reflection while listening to the stories of others. at the heart of the work is an audio booth that functions as both sanctuary and stage, where vulnerability becomes a form of resistance and truth is spoken without absolution.

the installation is accompanied by two printed works: a project manifesto that articulates the emotional and conceptual foundation of the piece, and a zine — the holy bible of homosexuality — which reimagines religious aesthetics through the lens of queerness, repression, beauty, and survival. together, these elements form a quiet but confrontational landscape that asks what it means to confess, to believe, and to exist honestly in spaces that were never built to hold us.
