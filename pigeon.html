berkeley pigeon project

humane urban wildlife design with the city of berkeley

the pigeon roosting mitigation project at the telegraph-channing garage transforms how urban spaces interact with wildlife. developed in collaboration with the city of berkeley's public works department, it takes humane, sustainable approaches to a common but poorly managed urban problem — one costing the city roughly $20,000 a year in cleaning and repairs.

historically, solutions like spikes, nets, and chemical deterrents have been deployed with varying success, often lacking compassion and long-term efficacy. our team developed three coordinated solutions: bird deterrent spikes replacing an outdated electric shock system, galvanized steel netting replacing failing nylon on the north-facing levels, and an ultrasonic predator deterrent system for hard-to-reach roof and gutter areas.

the goal is a paradigm shift in urban pest control — away from adversarial approaches, toward solutions that harmonize with nature.

community collaboration shaped the whole effort: working directly with berkeley residents and stakeholders ensured the implementations serve both the public and the wildlife ethically. the project aspires to set a new benchmark for how cities integrate humane wildlife management into their infrastructure.
