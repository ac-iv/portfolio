richmond tent encampment

design with, not for, an unhoused community

the tent encampment project in richmond is a real, ongoing initiative developed in collaboration with community partners. inspired by the principles of andrew heben's tent city urbanism, it seeks to create a more humane and sustainable living environment for unhoused individuals — a hands-on approach to rethinking urban encampments around the immediate and long-term needs of the community.

the design process emphasizes flexibility, adaptability, and resourcefulness, using both traditional and innovative methods to establish a supportive, functional community space. residents are involved in the decision-making process, giving them ownership and agency over their environment — addressing basic needs while fostering dignity and hope.

by working directly with local organizations and stakeholders, the project aspires to set a new standard for how encampments can be thoughtfully designed to promote social equity, sustainability, and community-driven development — combining the power of design with grassroots activism.
